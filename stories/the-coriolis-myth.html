// 01. THE POPULAR PARADOX

A Southern Hemisphere Swirl?

The myth has been broadcast in cartoons, travel shows, and high-school classrooms: pull the plug in Australia, and the water flows clockwise; do it in America, and it turns counter-clockwise.

It sounds beautifully neat, connecting our daily household activities with the grand cosmic ballet of planetary motion. But fluid dynamics tells a completely different story.

At the scale of a domestic sink or toilet, the Coriolis force is so infinitesimal that it is utterly overwhelmed by everyday factors: the shape of the basin, the angle of the faucet, the residual motion left over from filling the sink, and even microscopic irregularities in the drain hole itself.

The Rossby Number Calculator

Physicists use the non-dimensional Rossby Number (Ro) to determine if Coriolis matters. If Ro << 1, Earth's spin dictates the fluid. If Ro >> 1, the Coriolis force is a rounding error.

// 03. PLANETARY WEATHER SIMULATION

The Earth in Motion

On a global scale, wind currents rushing towards low-pressure centers are deflected over huge distances. Because Earth rotates eastward beneath this moving air, paths curve.

In the Northern Hemisphere, deflection is to the right (creating counter-clockwise rotation around low pressure). In the Southern Hemisphere, it's to the left.

01 / GEOMETRIC ACCELERATION

Drain Geometry

Sinks and toilets are manufactured with micro-scale physical imperfections. Even a tiny 1-millimeter asymmetry acts as an aerodynamic ramp, directing water with magnitudes of force far eclipsing Coriolis.

02 / INERTIAL LEGACY

Residual Motion

Water poured into a sink remains rotating slightly for up to 24 hours. Unless a basin sits in perfectly vibration-free isolators for days, the lingering kinetic energy from filling the sink completely dictates its drain spiral.

03 / REAL LAB EXPERIMENTS

The Extreme Protocol

In 1962, physicist Ascher Shapiro tested this. He used a perfect 2-meter bowl, filled it with settled water for 24 hours, and drained it under strictly controlled lab conditions. Only then did he prove Coriolis worked on a basin.
