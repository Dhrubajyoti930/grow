The Illusion of Time

You think you perceive the world in real-time. You don't. Your brain is a master editor, stitching together sensory data with a slight delay, rearranging the sequence to make sense of a chaotic universe.

This is known as Chronostasis. When you flick your eyes from one point to another, your brain freezes the image input to avoid the blur of motion. It backfills the gap, making it seem as if the clock ticked longer than it actually did.
