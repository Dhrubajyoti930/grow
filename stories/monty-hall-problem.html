Why 66%?

When you first pick, you have a 1/3 chance of being right. The other 2/3 of the probability is concentrated in the two doors you didn't pick.

When Monty opens a goat door from that remaining group, he isn't changing the original odds: that 2/3 probability stays trapped in the unpicked set. Switching effectively "collects" all that probability into the remaining closed door.
